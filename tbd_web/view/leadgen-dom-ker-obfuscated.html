About Kerala

Discover God's Own Country

Nestled in the lush landscapes of South India, Kerala is a tranquil paradise known for its serene backwaters, spice-scented hills, pristine beaches, and vibrant cultural traditions. From the colonial charm of Cochin to the misty hills of Munnar, the wilderness of Thekkady to the romantic houseboats of Alleppey, Kerala offers a journey that blends nature, heritage, and hospitality like nowhere else.

Come discover why Kerala is fondly called "God's Own Country" - where every moment is a celebration of life, nature, and the rich cultural tapestry that makes this destination truly unique.

Kerala Attractions

Explore the best that Kerala has to offer

Cochin Fort Heritage Tour

Visit the historic sites of Fort Cochin including the Dutch Palace, Jewish Synagogue, Santa Cruz Basilica, and the iconic Chinese Fishing Nets.

Munnar's Tea Gardens & National Park

Explore lush tea estates, Eravikulam National Park (home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr), and enjoy a serene mountain landscape.

Mattupetty & Echo Point, Munnar

Enjoy panoramic views at Mattupetty Dam and shout your heart out at Echo Point—an acoustic marvel nestled in the hills.

Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, Thekkady

Take a peaceful boat ride in Periyar Lake surrounded by dense forests and wildlife, followed by a spice plantation tour.

Cultural Evening in Thekkady

Get a taste of Kerala's rich heritage with traditional Kathakali or Kalaripayattu shows in the evening.

Alleppey Backwater Houseboat Experience

Sail through palm-lined canals in a deluxe houseboat, enjoy village views, and feast on traditional meals as you float through "The Venice of the East."

Your 6-Day Kerala Itinerary

A carefully crafted journey to experience the best of Kerala

Day 1: Arrive in Cochin | Half-day heritage sightseeing

Upon arrival at Cochin International Airport, you'll be greeted by our representative who will assist with your transfer to the hotel. After check-in, embark on a half-day heritage tour of Fort Cochin, visiting the Dutch Palace, Jewish Synagogue, and the iconic Chinese Fishing Nets.

Day 2: Drive to Munnar | Visit tea gardens en route | Explore Eravikulam National Park

After breakfast, drive to the picturesque hill station of Munnar. En route, visit tea gardens and enjoy the scenic beauty. Upon arrival, explore the Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr and stunning mountain landscapes.

Day 3: Full-day Munnar sightseeing including Tea Museum, Echo Point & more

Spend the day exploring Munnar's attractions including the Tea Museum, Mattupetty Dam, Echo Point, and panoramic viewpoints. Enjoy the cool mountain air and breathtaking vistas of the Western Ghats.

Day 4: Proceed to Thekkady | Boating at Periyar Lake | Spice tour & cultural show

Drive to Thekkady and enjoy a peaceful boat ride on Periyar Lake surrounded by dense forests and wildlife. Later, visit a spice plantation to learn about Kerala's famous spices, followed by an evening of traditional Kathakali or Kalaripayattu performances.

Day 5: Travel to Alleppey | Houseboat cruise & stay

Journey to Alleppey, the "Venice of the East," and board your traditional houseboat for an unforgettable backwater experience. Cruise through palm-lined canals, enjoy village views, and feast on authentic Kerala cuisine as you float through the serene backwaters.

Day 6: Return to Cochin | Shopping if time permits | Departure

After breakfast on the houseboat, return to Cochin. If time permits, enjoy some shopping for local souvenirs and spices. Later, transfer to Cochin International Airport for your departure with memories of a lifetime.
